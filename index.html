All About Dairy Milk

History of Dairy Milk

Dairy Milk, a brand of milk chocolate manufactured by Cadbury, was first introduced in 1905 in the United Kingdom. The initial recipe consisted of a higher proportion of milk than its competitors, making it stand out in the market. Over the years, Dairy Milk has become one of the best-selling chocolates globally, known for its smooth and creamy texture. The iconic purple packaging, introduced in 1920, has become synonymous with the brand. Today, Dairy Milk is available in over 30 countries, beloved by chocolate enthusiasts worldwide.

Types of Dairy Milk

Classic Dairy Milk: The original milk chocolate bar, available in sizes ranging from small individual bars to large family packs.

Dairy Milk Fruit & Nut: Combines the classic milk chocolate with pieces of raisins and almonds.

Dairy Milk Caramel: Features a smooth caramel filling encased in Dairy Milk chocolate.

Dairy Milk Whole Nut: Packed with whole hazelnuts for a crunchy texture.

Dairy Milk Oreo: A blend of Dairy Milk chocolate and Oreo cookie pieces.

Dairy Milk Silk: A premium variant offering an extra smooth and silky chocolate experience.

Interesting Facts or Trivia

  • Oldest Brand: Dairy Milk is one of the oldest and most recognizable chocolate brands in the world.
  • World Record: In 2014, Cadbury created the world’s largest chocolate bar, weighing nearly 5,792.50 kg (12,770 lb) as part of Dairy Milk's promotional campaign.
  • Iconic Color: The distinctive purple color of Dairy Milk's packaging is trademarked, reflecting its rich and royal heritage.
  • Fairtrade Commitment: Since 2009, Cadbury has committed to sourcing Fairtrade cocoa for Dairy Milk, supporting sustainable farming practices and fair wages for farmers.
  • Cultural Impact: Dairy Milk has been a part of many cultural moments, from memorable advertisements to featuring in movies and TV shows.
